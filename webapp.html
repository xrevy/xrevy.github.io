Ginflut

This university project was made in a group of 3. The main task was to implement a user system and a sql database in a shop-like environment. So we decided to create a Gin Shop called "Ginflut".

Users have the opportunity to sign up as a seller to create item pages and upload details and pictures of their products. Other than that, users can write comments of products to rate them.
The backend was made in PHP and JavaScript, general functionality is written in JavaScript aswell. For the styling we used mainly bootstrap, but the big showcase carousel on the landing page was made by us.
The logo of Ginflut was also made by one of our group members.

Figma Prototyping - Apex Legends Gambling Addiction Protection

This university project involved using the prototyping application Figma. The task for this exercise was to create a figma prototype that lets the user don't want to use an application.

A lot of games offers players to buy so-called loot boxes that contain random items that the player can use to change the appearance of things in video games. All items have a different rarity, most of the time players will get common loot out of that loot boxes. The items most players want are pretty rare to get, requiring the player to buy more loot boxes to gamble for a better item.
Since games that involve purchasable loot boxes are played by really young people as well, they might develop a insensitivity when it comes to gambling and spend more money than wanted or allowed.
Apex Legends is a First Person Battle Royal Shooter that also offers players to buy loot boxes ("Apex Packs"). Since Apex Legends is played by younger people as well. To draw attention towards said issue, I tried to make a prototype that creates more hurdles before buying Apex Packs. The player has to conform their age, that they are even eligible for gambling and if they are old enough, they have to go through an gambling addiction test. These instances and many warnings about the purchase and spending money should make the player question their decision about spending real money to a game for random cosmetical digital items.
The video was edited in Adobe AfterFX.
